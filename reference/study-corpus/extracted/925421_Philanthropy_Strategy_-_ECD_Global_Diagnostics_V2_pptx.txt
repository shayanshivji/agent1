What is ECD? Early Childhood Development (ECD)  refers to the  holistic development of children from pregnancy to age 8 , spanning physical, cognitive, emotional, social, and language development These  early years lay the foundation  for lifelong well-being, learning, and future success
Social protection:  Protects children from violence, neglect, and family separation, while promoting legal identity, psychosocial support, inclusive child benefits, and local governance for child well-being (e.g., birth registration, safe public spaces) ECD framework is built on 4 key pillars with 2 cross-cutting dimensions impacting early childhood core pillars ECD Overview: How are we faring on meeting children and family needs Health:  Ensures healthy development through maternal and child healthcare, early intervention, and illness prevention (e.g., neonatal screening, high immunization coverage) Education:  Supports cognitive and emotional development through quality early education, nurturing care, and home learning environments (e.g., access to preschool with play-based learning, engaged caregivers at home) Urban planning &  design Climate & sustainability Health Education Nutrition Social protection Nutrition:  Supports physical and cognitive development through adequate maternal and child nutrition, breastfeeding promotion and monitoring of growth and diet quality (e.g., micronutrient supplementation, nutrition counseling) Climate & sustainability:  Building climate-resilient early childhood systems by integrating sustainability, adaptation, and disaster preparedness across all services (e.g., climate-informed curriculum, safe water and sanitation access) Urban planning & design:  Creating safe, accessible, and stimulating environments that promote young children's healthy development, learning, and caregiver well-being (e.g., bus stops with early learning prompts, traffic-calmed zones around pre-schools) Source: UNICEF, UNESCO, Van Leer, WHO, LSE
