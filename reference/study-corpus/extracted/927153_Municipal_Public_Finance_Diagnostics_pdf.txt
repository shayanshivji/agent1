Context Objectives
This document resulted from an engagement to 
analyze revenue and expenditure growth and 
identify potential revenue opportunities in a major 
US city
As cities experience population growth and face 
rising costs, they must grow their revenue in order 
to maintain and improve their infrastructure, services, 
and overall quality of life for residents
Peer benchmarking can reveal how a given city’s 
revenue breakdown compares to peers in major 
revenue categories (e.g., tax, licenses and permits, 
intergovernmental aid)
Outside-in sizing of revenue levers leveraged by 
peer cities can help identify potential pockets of 
untapped opportunity
To share the approach we used in this project, as a 
starting place for teams serving other municipal 
government clients on similar fiscal health topics
To share sample analyses and tips and tricks 
across the three analytical phases of this work:
• Analyze historical revenues and expenditures 
and understand growth drivers
• Benchmark revenue levers across key 
categories relative to peers 
• Identify and size impact of potential revenue 
levers
Note this document is NOT an exhaustive set of analyses and is not intended to 
suggest any strategic posture or drive decision making
