Offering deep-dives>>>
A. Commercial Win Room: Growth Levers Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Rapidly  size and prioritize growth opportunities  within the client’s existing customer and product base Identify where  sales coverage or customer retention gaps  are limiting growth potential Provide a  fact pack for leadership  to shape the commercial agenda and inform immediate sales actions Analyze  24–36 months of transactional sales data  to uncover growth drivers and lost opportunities through a two-week rapid diagnostic Conduct targeted analyses across key growth levers, including: Cross-sell / share of wallet:  identify underpenetrated segments across product categories Churn prevention:  identify customer retention issues based on declining volume and sales Rep performance:  assess cross-selling and hunting across sellers and regions Account coverage:  evaluate alignment of seller focus to account size Clear, quantified view of  growth at stake  across key levers Tangible evidence  to help prioritize potential actions (e.g., rep reallocation, account recovery, cross-sell campaigns) Serves as input to  broader commercial transformation or IDP opportunity sizing
B. Sales Navigator Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Benchmark  sales productivity  across KPIs for efficiency, cost, and organizational effectiveness Quantify the  value at stake  from commercial improvement opportunities Provide a  data-backed fact pack  to set  aspirational targets  and guide transformation priorities Analyze  Sales Navigator benchmarks from 400+ companies / 4,500+ business  entities across industries Compare client’s KPIs to peer sets  along productivity, efficiency, and cost levers Conduct diagnostic across core dimensions: Productivity  – revenue and gross profit per sales FTE, account, and enablement role Efficiency  – selling cost and marketing cost relative to revenue and gross profit Coverage & Organization  – sales span of control, account ownership, and enablement ratios Estimate top-down commercial improvement  potential via ROI driver tree Quantified opportunity  across levers (productivity, efficiency, coverage) with benchmarked targets Fact-based recommendations  to guide commercial program design and prioritization Supports IDP / broader transformation roadmap  through tangible, data-backed insights
C. Activity Analytics Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Create visibility into  how sellers spend their time  across customer, internal, and admin work Identify  productivity gaps and collaboration patterns  limiting commercial effectiveness Provide  data-backed insights  to reallocate sales time toward higher-value activities Use  anonymized email and calendar data  to measure time spent across activities. Link with  HR and performance data  to surface key productivity drivers Analyze across four dimensions and compare against 50+ benchmarks Sales time allocation:  quantify customer-facing vs. internal time Collaboration patterns:  assess meeting load and cross-functional engagement Customer coverage:  evaluate frequency and depth of account interactions Manager effectiveness:  review coaching cadence and 1-on-1 time Deliver a  fact base on sales productivity  and time use Identify  potential weekly seller capacity  that can be freed up Provide  evidence to refocus time  and improve collaboration and coaching
D. Sales DNA Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Diagnose the  capabilities, mindsets, and motivations  that drive frontline sales performance Identify  what differentiates top performers  and quantify the value of capability uplift Provide clients with a  fact base for talent strategy , hiring profiles, and coaching priorities Conduct a  ~60 minute, behavior-based self-assessment  across seven lenses:  Skills, Coaching, Time Allocation, Intrinsics, Motivations, Culture, and CRM usage Benchmark results against a  database of 60,000+ sales reps across ~180 companies  and multiple industries Link behavioral and capability data to performance metrics  (e.g., quota attainment, revenue, margin) to uncover high-impact talent levers Generates manager and rep insi ghts through correlations, heatmaps, and qualitative feedback Clear, quantified view of  growth at stake  across key levers Tangible evidence  to help prioritize potential actions (e.g., rep reallocation, account recovery, cross-sell campaigns) Serves as input to  broader commercial transformation or IDP opportunity sizing
E. Pipeline Analytics Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Assess  sales process performance  and identify  conversion gaps  across the funnel Determine whether the  pipeline is large and fast enough  to meet growth targets Provide  data-driven insights  to improve win rates, velocity, and forecast accuracy Use  CRM data exports  (e.g., Salesforce) to analyze opportunity flow from lead to close Map the  end-to-end customer journey  to pinpoint where deals stall or drop Evaluate performance through key metrics such as  win rate, pipeline coverage, stage velocity, and deal size Combine quantitative findings with  sales leader interviews  to contextualize results and identify root causes Deliver a clear view of  pipeline health and funnel efficiency Quantify  value-at-stake  from accelerating deals or improving stage conversion Provide  data for ideas  to boost conversion, shorten cycle time, and strengthen pipeline management discipline
F. Commercial Win Room: Pricing Levers Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Rapidly  identify and size pricing opportunities  within the client’s existing customer and product portfolio Create a  data-driven view of margin leakage  and value capture potential across key pricing dimensions Provide  leadership-ready insights  to prioritize where disciplined pricing execution can drive impact Analyze  24–36 months of transactional sales data  to assess pricing performance and uncover opportunity through a two-week rapid diagnostic Conduct targeted analyses across core pricing levers, including: Cost pass-through:  measure effectiveness of cost recovery and identify unrealized margin potential Margin variability:  assess inconsistency in pricing across similar customers, products, and regions Non-key value items:  pinpoint products where price premiums can be captured without volume risk Long tail optimization:  evaluate low-margin customers and products for targeted price action Clear, quantified view of  margin at stake  across key levers Tangible evidence  to help prioritize potential actions (e.g., cost pass-through, margin increase on the longtail, discount optimization) Serves as input to  broader commercial transformation or IDP opportunity sizing
G. CCAT Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Assess a  client’s sales capabilities against peers  to identify strengths, gaps, and improvement priorities Help commercial leaders  align on the maturity of core capabilities  and set a transformation roadmap Create a fact-based for capability-building programs  and broader commercial excellence initiatives Structured diagnostic across 12 commercial capabilities and enablers , covering strategy, go-to-market, sales, pricing, tools, and talent Delivered via survey (~30 min) or interviews , available in multiple languages Benchmarked against 270+ companies and ~220 best practices , with qualitative insights and a free-text comment book Optional deep dives  link perceptions, practices, and outcomes to clarify root causes  behind performance gaps Clear  capability maturity baseline and priority roadmap  to guide investment and focus Leadership alignment on the “so-what”  — where the organization truly lags or leads Actionable insights to strengthen talent, performance management, and commercial systems Often used as a  launchpad for broader commercial transformations  or capability academies
H. Time & Automation Scan Objective Approach Outcome Sample output Identify non–value-added activities  consuming seller time and  pinpoint automation opportunities  Quantify the time and value at stake  from freeing up sales capacity for growth or cost savings Build the fact base  for sales productivity initiatives, automation roadmaps, and CRM enablement programs Assess  85+ sales tasks from lead to invoice  using a seller survey and/or structured automation interviews Benchmark automation maturity against  50+ companies and best-in-class reference models Analyze time allocation  across roles, regions, and sales motions to  identify major “time sinks” Estimate value potential  by linking freed-up capacity to cost reduction or growth upside Generate an  initial initiative list of automation and task offloading opportunities View of  where seller time is spent  and  how much can be automated Quantified productivity potential  (typically 10–20% of sales time freed up) and  $ value at stake Prioritized automation roadmap  with 3–5 high-impact use cases and business case sizing Tangible input for tech readiness assessment or CRM transformation roadmap  (e.g., Salesforce enablement)
