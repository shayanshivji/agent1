# McKinsey Interview Frameworks (extracted)

Source decks in this folder. Used to ground Agent 1 prompt structure.

## Behavioral interview structure (920087)

**Three-part flow:**
- Intro (~5 min): welcome, outline agenda, set expectations (notes, probing)
- Core (~30 min): behavioral questions tied to criteria; STAR probes for depth
- Closing (~10 min): candidate questions, next steps

**STAR probing:**
- Situation/Task: objective, challenge, stakeholders
- Action: specific role (not "we"), decisions, who involved
- Result: outcome, impact, what they learned

**Follow-up triggers:** vague answers, opinions, hypotheticals, partial STARs, "false STARs"

**Interviewer principles:** assess against rubric only; no comparison to other candidates; score immediately; combat bias (affinity, halo, confirmation, recency)

## IDP executive diagnostic questions (895216)

Adapt for SME process interviews:
- How has this workflow/area performed over past 2–3 years? What drove that?
- Most significant changes tried — successful vs unsuccessful and why?
- Biggest opportunities for value capture in your area?
- What has held the team back from capturing those?
- Who else should we talk to?
- Biggest challenge in improving this process?
- What factors would make improvement successful?
- What can you tell us that no one else will?
- Where are you an outlier vs how things typically work?

## Competency-based selection logic (889050)

- Pre-defined criteria and question guide per role
- Main questions weighted by criteria; follow-ups only for evidence gaps
- 30 min core + structured scoring
- Three-step agenda: intro (5) → main interview (20) → close (5)
- Notes and scores captured per criterion during interview

## Full-stack / craft interview guide (917700)

- Questions for inspiration — not all required
- Let interviewee know you'll take notes and may redirect for efficiency
- Evaluate individually against bar, not relative to other candidates
- Multi-round structure: screen → technical → fit/leadership

## Application to BSN diagnostic (Agent 1)

Process/SME interviews are **not hiring interviews**, but the same principles apply:
- Open-ended, past-behavior questions ("walk me through the last time…")
- STAR-style probes when answers are vague
- Structured sections: objective, known vs confirm, questions, probes, evidence
- Intro/closeout blocks in every guide
- Score evidence (facts, metrics, examples) not impressions
